Bolognese

Best Homemade Bolognese Sauce Recipe

Bolognese sauce, or ragù alla bolognese, is a rich and flavorful meat sauce originating from Bologna, Italy. It's characterized by its slow-cooked mixture of ground meat, typically a blend of beef and pork, simmered in a flavorful sauce made with milk, cream, and a combination of vegetables like carrots, celery, and onions. This long cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a deeply satisfying sauce that's perfect for pairing with pasta like tagliatelle or lasagna.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup finely diced onion
  • ½ cup finely diced celery
  • ½ cup finely diced carrot
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 ½ pounds ground beef
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ cups 2% milk
  • 2 cups white wine
  • 1 (28 ounce) can whole Italian plum tomatoes (preferably San Marzano)
  • 2 cups water, or as needed
